How to Prepare for a Job Interview

Walk in like you own the place. Here's the game plan.

1. Research Like a Spy (But Legally)

You need to know the company better than their intern knows where the best breakroom snacks are. Understand the company’s mission, values, recent news, and exactly what they’re hiring for. Bonus: check LinkedIn for your interviewer’s vibe.

2. Know Your Résumé Like Your Own Netflix History

If it’s on your résumé, be ready to explain it. Use STAR format--Situation, Task, Action, Result--to talk about what you’ve done and why it mattered. No bluffing. No fluffing.

3. Prep Answers (But Don’t Sound Like a Robot)

Hit the common ones: "Tell me about yourself," "What’s your biggest weakness," "Why do you want this job?" Craft solid, honest responses with bullet points or short story arcs. Focus on results and self-awareness.

4. Ask Smart Questions

You’re interviewing them too. Avoid "how much PTO?" and instead ask about the team’s definition of success, challenges ahead, or how they define culture on hard days.

5. Dress Like You Already Work There

Match their vibe. Corporate? Go polished. Startup? Smart casual. Remote? Still put effort in--yes, even on Zoom. You’re not just showing up, you’re showing *up.*

6. Mock Interviews Aren’t Just for Nerds

Practice aloud. Record yourself. Ask a friend to grill you. You’ll sound smoother and spot habits you didn’t know you had.

7. Day-Of: Control What You Can

Sleep well, eat something, and show up 10–15 minutes early. Bring copies of your résumé or have your digital setup ready. Don’t wing it--win it.

8. Follow Up Like a Human

Send a thank-you email within 24 hours. Mention something specific. Be brief, be grateful, be real.

Bottom Line

You don’t need to be perfect. You need to be prepared, present, and unapologetically you. Know your value and bring it. Let them see why you're the best thing that could happen to their team.
