Follow-Up Like a Pro

The interview’s over. You did the thing. Now finish strong with a follow-up that seals the deal.

1. Send a Thank-You Email Within 24 Hours

Short, clear, and human. Mention something specific from the convo and restate your interest. Keep it under 150 words--tight and personal always wins.

2. Sample Template

Dear [Interviewer’s Name], Thank you for the conversation today--it was a pleasure to learn more about the [Job Title] role and the work your team is doing. I especially enjoyed our discussion about [specific topic]. I'm even more excited about the opportunity to contribute and bring my [your strength] to the table. Please let me know if you need anything else from me. Best, [Your Name]

3. Didn't Hear Back After a Week?

Ping them--nicely. You’re showing interest, not being needy. Say: "Just checking in to see if there’s an update on the role. I’m still very interested and happy to provide anything else you might need."

4. Use This Follow-Up Nudge

Hi [Interviewer’s Name], Hope you're well! Just wanted to follow up on the [Job Title] position I interviewed for on [Date]. I really appreciated the chance to speak with you and learn more about your team. Still very interested in the role--just checking in to see if there's any update. Thanks again, [Your Name]

5. Keep the Connection Warm

Even if you don’t get the job, the relationship can still be valuable. A simple, "Thanks for the update--I’d love to stay in touch," keeps the door open for future opportunities. Professional, calm, mature.

TL;DR

Follow-up isn’t desperate--it’s strategic. Done right, it reminds them you exist, you care, and you’ve got your act together. Be memorable. Be gracious. Be the kind of person people want to work with.
